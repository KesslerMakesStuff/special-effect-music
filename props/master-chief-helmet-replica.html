Behind the Build

How this piece came together

I have had a deep love for the Halo franchise since the very first Halo game release. I've always wanted Master Chiefs helmet for my prop collection, so I decided it was time to take on this project.

I bought the 3D model from Galactic Armory and printed the helmet in parts out of PLA+. I then epoxied those parts together and used a mix of sanding, filling primer, and body filler to get the helmet as smooth as possible.

From there I mixed and tested green paint until I got as close to the original Master Chief helmet color as I could. I then weathered the helmet using a mix of scratching, a water/spray paint technique, and some "artistic rough housing".

For the vents I 3D printed in TPU to give them a rubber-like texture. For the visor I made a buck out of the 3D model provided by Galactic Armory and used my home built vacuum forming machine to mold PETG around it. I then dyed the plastic using iDye Poly orange and airbrushed a layer of Spaz Stix mirror chrome on the inside to create the opaque look of the original helmet. All of this was glued onto the helmet with hot glue so it can be removed easily if needed down the road.

Finally I added LEDs to the light holes on the helmet, wired to a AA battery pack glued to the inside. I used floor protection dots to diffuse the light from the LEDs. I also added fans to keep the helmet cool and prevent the visor from fogging, with vents throughout the helmet to move the air. All of the electronics were glued in and connected to a portable battery.

Techniques Used

FDM printing in PLA+ and TPU Epoxy bonding Sanding Custom color mixing and testing Spray painting Airbrushing Water/spray paint weathering technique Physical distressing Weathering Plastic dyeing Vacuum Forming LED wiring and installation Light diffusion Fan installation Battery pack wiring and mounting
